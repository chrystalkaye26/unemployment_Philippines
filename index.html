Unemployment in the Philippines

"Over 2 million Filipinos are jobless in 2024."

What is Unemployment?

Unemployment refers to the situation where individuals who are capable of working and are actively seeking a job cannot find one.

As of 2024, the unemployment rate in the Philippines remains a key concern for the economy.

About the Issue

Unemployment affects not just individuals but entire families and communities. It leads to reduced income, lower spending, and slower economic growth.

Causes of Unemployment

  • Lack of job opportunities
  • Mismatch between skills and job requirements
  • Economic downturns
  • Automation and technological changes

Effects of Unemployment

  • Poverty and reduced quality of life
  • Increase in mental health issues
  • Brain drain or migration
  • Slower national development

Proposed Solutions / Social Change

To address unemployment, we can:

  • Invest in skills training and education
  • Support small businesses and entrepreneurship
  • Encourage foreign investments to create jobs
  • Enhance job matching programs
